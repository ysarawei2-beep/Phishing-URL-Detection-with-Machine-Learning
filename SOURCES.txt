----------------------------------------------------------------
2. ACADEMIC PAPERS
----------------------------------------------------------------
[P1] Sahoo, D., Liu, C., & Hoi, S. C. H. (2017).
     "Malicious URL Detection using Machine Learning: A Survey."
     arXiv:1701.07179.
     Used for the selection of lexical features and the list of
     suspicious phishing keywords in
     src/feature_engineering/url_features.py.

[P2] Aleroud, A., & Zhou, L. (2017).
     "Phishing Environments, Techniques, and Countermeasures: A
      Survey." Computers & Security, 68, 160–196.
     Motivated the list of URL-shortener services checked by the
     feature extractor.

[P3] Marchal, S., Saari, K., Singh, N., & Asokan, N. (2016).
     "Know Your Phish: Novel Techniques for Detecting Phishing
      Sites and their Targets." IEEE ICDCS.
     Motivation for the "suspicious TLDs" list.

[P4] Hannousse, A., & Yahiouche, S. (2021).
     "Towards Benchmark Datasets for Machine Learning Based
      Website Phishing Detection: An Experimental Study."
     Engineering Applications of AI, 104, 104347.
     Reference for choosing Random Forest as a strong baseline
     on URL feature datasets.

[P5] Verma, R., & Das, A. (2017).
     "What's in a URL: Fast Feature Extraction and Malicious URL
      Detection." Proc. IWSPA 2017.
     Reference for lexical-feature engineering.
